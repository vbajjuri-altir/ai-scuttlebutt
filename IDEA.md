# AI Scuttlebutt

> *Scuttlebutt* — naval slang for intelligence gathered from informal sources. Warren Buffett's term for his channel-checking research method.

## The Problem

Researching a startup today is a multi-hour, multi-tab slog. You have to:

1. Hunt across Crunchbase, PitchBook, Tracxn, MCA, Zauba Corp for financials and funding data
2. Read through G2, Glassdoor, Reddit, App Store reviews to understand product sentiment
3. Manually assess whether the company has a defensible MOAT (network effects, switching costs, IP, etc.)
4. Deep-dive on founders — LinkedIn, past ventures, press coverage, red flags
5. Stitch all of it together into a coherent investment thesis or competitive brief

This takes a skilled analyst 4–8 hours. Even then, it's incomplete and inconsistent.

## The Insight

AI is exceptional at two things that are core to this workflow:
- **Gathering** — crawling, querying, and aggregating public data from dozens of sources at once
- **Summarising** — synthesising noisy, contradictory information into clear, structured takeaways

## The Solution: AI Scuttlebutt

A tool that takes a company name (or URL) as input and produces a full **Startup Intelligence Report** in minutes, covering:

- **Financial pulse** — funding rounds, burn estimates, revenue signals, MCA/ROC filings (India)
- **Product intelligence** — tech stack, changelog, job listings as R&D signals
- **Customer sentiment** — aggregated reviews across G2, Glassdoor, App Store, Reddit
- **MOAT analysis** — AI-assessed defensibility across 5 dimensions
- **Founder fingerprint** — backgrounds, track records, patterns, red flags
- **Red flag radar** — legal issues, mass layoffs, fake reviews, pivot history

## Who It's For

- **Angel investors & VCs** — rapid pre-meeting diligence
- **Startup analysts** — competitive intelligence at scale
- **Founders** — researching competitors and market maps
- **PE/Corp Dev teams** — M&A target screening

## Why Now

- LLMs can now reliably extract and reason over public web data
- OSINT sources (Crunchbase, MCA, LinkedIn, reviews) have stable APIs and scraping surfaces
- There's no AI-native, end-to-end startup research tool in the market yet

## Tagline

**"Know everything about any startup. In minutes, not days."**
